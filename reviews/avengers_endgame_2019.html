Avengers: Endgame

April 26, 2019 - 11:59 PM - Digital IMAX

It's an act of letting go.

I am ... Iron Man.

Spoilers Ahead

The first thing that crossed my mind when watching the film was: Avengers: Endgame is a love letter to the fans of the MCU. For all of us who have seen these characters grow, fail, and overcome obstacles over the past ten years, this movie makes us feel right at home.

Yet, the genius of the film is its ability to thrust us into the lives of these characters and feel like we can control their destinies - when we can’t. My personal favorites and the figureheads of the MCU - Steve Rogers and Tony Stark - speak to their friends in the film saying things like Move on or Part of the journey is the end. But, even if audiences don’t realize it, you know the filmmakers are speaking through the characters to reach us.

And that’s part of Endgame. It’s an act of letting go. It’s tough on the heart, but it feels natural. Sometimes, stories don’t have happy endings. Especially when the greatest villain the Avengers have ever known still rules the universe.

There’s so many dots to be connected. One of the best examples is Tony’s sacrifice. There’s only one outcome where the Avengers win, according to Dr. Strange. It only happens if Tony uses the Infinity Gauntlet. Tony knows what would happen if he didn’t. He saw in Age of Ultron when the vision of his fallen comrades surrounds him and Cap mutters, You could’ve saved us. I know I’ve left so many on the table, but this movie is dense and it all fits together. Props to Kevin Feige.

It is the end of an era. The original six Avengers who have collectively guided me through elementary, middle, and high school are no more. “Endgame” puts the entire saga into perspective, but I also see that I will savor their journeys even more. It’s a marvelous capstone on a series that was too-good-to-be-true in the first place.
