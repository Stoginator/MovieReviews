Captain America: Civil War

May 15, 2016 - 3:13 PM - Standard

This is the movie that made me hate Iron Man (briefly).

She's just a kid!

This is the movie that made me hate Iron Man. Civil War blurs the line between right and wrong, good and bad, whatever it is. It casts a gray shadow over everything and just when it is resolved and the characters see, questions and answers are thrown back again. That's what great movies do, they twist your opinions so freely, that you don't know how you believed what you did in the first place.

Hovering over a great political argument about regulating the Avengers and holding them accountable from a viewpoint never expressed before is powerful. It is allegorical of a modern-day debate over security versus freedom, but this time it is extremely, utterly personal, overlapping from the heroes (?) to us. To tackle such a goal as complex as this is commendable and results from strong source material to an arguably stronger script.

You could classify Civil War as a psychological thriller, or an action movie, or even an action thriller, but the point is that it can be any of these. It mixes the genres well because it has a packed story that will keep the audience engaged until even after the credits ðŸ˜œ.
